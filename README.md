# NCCN Guideline Panel Simulator — Haematologic Malignancy Knowledge Reference

This document is the authoritative reference for the NCCN Guideline Panel Simulator agent. It contains verified historical precedents from NCCN B-Cell Lymphoma panel transparency documents (2021–2024), empirically derived calibration data, and sourced process information. Every precedent in this document is traceable to a specific NCCN source document.

**Scope:** This knowledge base covers haematologic malignancies with a deep focus on B-cell lymphoma panel decisions. The patterns and calibration anchors are derived from 93 real panel decisions (68 inclusions, 5 rejections) documented in NCCN transparency files from 2021–2024.

---

## Section 1: NCCN Panel Process


### Panel Meeting Structure
- Panels meet for **annual updates** (Version 1.YYYY) and **interim updates** (V.2, V.3, etc.) throughout the year.
- Interim meetings are held for new FDA approvals, expanded indications, or major new data from medical meetings.
- At least **50% panel attendance** is required for quorum.
- Panel leadership (Chair and Vice-Chair) moderate meetings, set agendas, and enforce conflict-of-interest recusals.

### Evidence Submission
- External submissions must be received **at least 4 weeks** prior to the scheduled annual panel meeting.
- All external submissions are made publicly available on the NCCN website.
- A **transparency document** is published with each guideline update capturing: changes made, submission requests, outcomes, references, and **aggregate voting results**.

### Conflict of Interest
- Panel members with marketing-focused industry activities or equity in the drug under deliberation (or competitors) **must recuse** from presentations, discussions, and voting.
- Research funding is excluded from conflict limits (goes to institutions, not individuals).
- Each panel includes at least one **patient advocate** as a full member.

### Categories of Evidence and Consensus
- Category 1: Based on high-level evidence, there is uniform NCCN consensus that the intervention is appropriate.
- Category 2A: Based on lower-level evidence, there is uniform NCCN consensus (≥85% support of the panel) that the intervention is appropriate.
- Category 2B: Based on lower-level evidence, there is NCCN consensus that the intervention is appropriate, but without uniform consensus.
- Category 3: Based on any level of evidence, there is major NCCN disagreement that the intervention is appropriate.
- Most NCCN recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated.

## Section 2: FDA-to-NCCN Timing Analysis (Verified)

*Source: Rios C, Haslam A, Prasad V. Journal of Cancer Policy, 2026 (open access: sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213538326000172). Analysis of 83 FDA approvals and 8 withdrawals, October 2022–July 2024.*

### Empirical Timing Data
- **Median time from FDA approval to NCCN update: 19 days**
- Traditional (regular) approvals: median 20 days
- Accelerated approvals: median 16 days
- No statistically significant difference between regular and accelerated approval timing
- **22% of cases: NCCN updated BEFORE FDA approval** (anticipating approvals based on published data)
- **Only 1.2%** of FDA-approved treatments in NCCN guidelines lacked uniform consensus (were Category 2B)

### Timing by Scenario (Derived from Published Analysis)

| Scenario | Expected NCCN Update Timeline |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| Major FDA approval with strong phase III data | 1–3 weeks |
| FDA accelerated approval | 2–4 weeks |
| New data at ASCO/ASH/ESMO (no FDA action) | Next scheduled panel meeting |
| Off-label evidence submission | Next scheduled annual meeting (may be deferred) |
| Confirmatory phase III after prior single-arm inclusion | 2–6 weeks for category upgrade |

## Section 4: Decision Pattern Analysis (Derived from Verified Data)

### Pattern 1: FDA-Approved Drug with Accelerated Approval (Single-Arm Data)

**Observed behaviour:**  
When an FDA-approved therapy with accelerated approval (often based on single-arm data) is reviewed by the NCCN B-cell lymphoma panel, it is typically incorporated into the guidelines despite lower-level evidence, particularly in relapsed/refractory settings with high unmet need.

**Typical outcome:**  
Most commonly **Category 2A** (lower-level evidence with uniform NCCN consensus).  
Preference designation (preferred vs other recommended) varies depending on available alternatives, comparative evidence, and clinical experience.

**Examples from database:**
- Epcoritamab (accelerated approval; EPCORE NHL-1 single-arm study): Category 2A  
- Glofitamab (accelerated approval; single-arm study): Category 2A  
- Pirtobrutinib (accelerated approval; BRUIN single-arm study): Category 2A  
- Loncastuximab tesirine (accelerated approval; single-arm study): Category 2A  

**Interpretation:**  
This pattern indicates that NCCN panels frequently consider single-arm evidence sufficient for clinical inclusion (Category 2A), even where confirmatory randomised evidence is not yet available.

### Pattern 2: Category Upgrade After Confirmatory Phase III Data

**Observed behaviour:** Drugs initially included at Category 2A based on single-arm or early data are upgraded to Category 1 when phase III RCT data confirms benefit.

**Examples from database:**
- **Liso-cel in 2L DLBCL:** Initially Cat 2A (2022) → Upgraded to **Cat 1** after TRANSFORM phase III (2023). Vote increased from 87.5% to 95.8%.
- **Pola-R-CHP in 1L DLBCL:** Cat 1, Preferred at 89.7% based on POLARIX phase III.
- **Axi-cel in 2L DLBCL:** Cat 1 at 78.3% based on ZUMA-7 phase III.

**Typical upgrade timeline:** 6–18 months from initial inclusion to upgrade, triggered by phase III publication or FDA conversion to full approval.

### Pattern 3: Off-Label Inclusion with High Consensus

**Observed behaviour:** The B-cell lymphoma panel is comfortable including therapies off-label when clinical rationale is strong.

**Examples from database:**
- Acalabrutinib for MZL: 100% vote, Preferred — no specific FDA approval for MZL
- Pirtobrutinib for MZL: 100% vote, Preferred — no FDA approval in MZL
- R-mini-CHOP for HGBL in frail/elderly: 100% vote, Cat 2A — clinical consensus without RCT

### Pattern 4: Category 2B — Genuine Panel Disagreement

**Observed behaviour:** Category 2B is assigned when vote falls between ~50-84%. In the database, 7 decisions received 2B with an average vote of 56%.

**Examples from database:**
- Liso-cel for DLBCL 2L (relapsed >12mo): 70.8% — 2B (later upgraded to 2A)
- Venetoclax/lenalidomide/rituximab for MCL: 58.6% — 2B
- Pola-R-CHP for HGBL double-hit: 56% — 2B (panel divided on extrapolation)
- Alternate BTKi substitution in TRIANGLE: 58.6% — 2B (no RCT data for substitution)
- RCEPP for DLBCL in frail elderly: 48.3% and 44.8% — 2B (both category downgrades from 2A)

**Key insight:** 2B commonly arises when evidence is being extrapolated from one population to another (e.g., specific drug to class substitution, one histology to another) without direct supporting data.

### Pattern 5: Preference Level Dynamics

**Observed behaviour from 68 inclusions:**
- Preferred: 11 decisions (16.2%)
- Other Recommended: 11 decisions (16.2%)
- Useful in Certain Circumstances: 1 decision (1.5%)
- Not specified/N/A: 45 decisions (66.2%)

**Drivers of "Preferred" status:**
- Phase III data showing superiority over existing SOC (Pola-R-CHP in 1L DLBCL: Cat 1, Preferred)
- Bispecific antibodies in transformed DLBCL (high unmet need, novel mechanism)
- BTK inhibitors with better safety profiles replacing older agents (zanubrutinib, acalabrutinib replacing ibrutinib)

**Drivers of "Other Recommended" status:**
- FDA-approved but without head-to-head superiority data vs existing options
- Drugs with concerning toxicity profiles relative to alternatives

## Section 5: B-Cell Lymphoma Panel Behavioural Profile

*Derived from 93 verified decisions, 2021–2024*

### Panel Characteristics
- **Panel size:** Typically 25–32 members (varies by meeting; 3–11 absent per meeting)
- **Meeting frequency:** Annual meeting + typically 4–6 guideline versions per year (based on source document registry: 4 versions in 2021, 5 in 2022, 6 in 2023)
- **Voting patterns:** Strong tendency toward consensus. 59% of all decisions had ≥90% agreement.
- **Rejection rate:** Very low (5.4%). Panel strongly prefers to include therapies, even with qualifications.

### Behavioural Tendencies
- **Comfortable with single-arm data:** Bispecific antibodies (epcoritamab, glofitamab) and CAR-T therapies were all included based on single-arm phase I/II data with FDA accelerated approval.
- **Rapid responder:** Bispecific antibodies were added within weeks of FDA approval, consistent with the published 19-day median.
- **Off-label comfort:** High. 58.8% of inclusions were for non-FDA-approved indications.
- **Safety-conscious:** Ibrutinib was unanimously downgraded from Preferred to Other Recommended based on cardiac toxicity data, replaced by zanubrutinib and acalabrutinib.
- **Extrapolation caution:** Panel is comfortable with a drug in its approved indication but more cautious when extrapolating to different histologies or substituting one drug for another within a class without RCT data (see TRIANGLE BTKi substitution: 2B at 58.6%).
- **Category upgrade track record:** Willing to upgrade categories when confirmatory data arrives (liso-cel 2A→1, Pola-R-CHP to Cat 1).

## Section 6: Evidence Quality Evaluation Framework

### Study Design Assessment
- **Strong:** Phase III RCT, well-powered, active comparator
- **Moderate:** Phase II RCT, large single-arm trial (n>100)
- **Weak:** Phase I, small single-arm (n<50), retrospective, case series

### Primary Endpoint Hierarchy (Haematologic Malignancies)
- **OS** remains the gold standard but is difficult to demonstrate in indolent NHL
- **PFS** is the most common phase III endpoint and is well-accepted by panels
- **ORR + CR rate** are the basis for most accelerated approvals; panels accept these when responses are durable
- **CR rate and MRD negativity** carry particular weight in haem-onc (more so than solid tumours)
- **DOR:** Median DOR ≥12 months is generally considered durable in aggressive lymphomas (interpretive; no official NCCN threshold exists)

### Effect Magnitude Thresholds (Interpretive — Not Official NCCN Thresholds)

*Note: NCCN does not publish specific numerical thresholds for effect magnitude. The following are interpretive ranges derived from the patterns observed in the decision database and general oncology practice. Use as rough guidance only.*

- **Strong:** ORR ≥60%, CR ≥30%, PFS HR ≤0.70
- **Moderate:** ORR 40–60%, CR 15–30%, PFS HR 0.70–0.80
- **Weak:** ORR <40%, CR <15%, PFS HR >0.80

## Section 8: Important Caveats

1. **This database covers B-cell lymphoma panel decisions (2021–2024).** Other haem-onc panels (AML, CLL, myeloma) may have different behavioural patterns, though the categorical framework is identical.

2. **Voting percentages do not always align with category assignments.** The panel may assign Cat 1 with <85% vote if the evidence itself is high-level (see axi-cel ZUMA-7: 78.3% vote, Cat 1). Conversely, Cat 2A can be assigned at votes below 85% (see umbralisib elderly/infirm FL: 81% vote, Cat 2A).

3. **Preference changes can pass at lower thresholds.** Tafasitamab + lenalidomide achieved Preferred at 51.7%. Preference-level votes appear to have different dynamics than new inclusion votes.

4. **Panel composition varies by meeting.** Absent members ranged from 3 to 11 across meetings, materially affecting vote percentages.

5. **The B-cell panel is among the most active NCCN panels**, with typically 5–8 guideline versions per year. Other panels may update less frequently.

6. **This is a simulation, not a prediction.** Actual panel decisions involve clinical judgment, interpersonal dynamics, and real-time discussion that cannot be fully captured.

7. **Cost considerations** are not formally part of category assignments but are beginning to influence preference designations in some panels (notably CML).

8. **NCCN Compendium listing** follows guideline recommendations and is critical for off-label reimbursement coverage by CMS and commercial payers.
